Welcome to the Samarkand Travel Guide, your starting point to explore one of Uzbekistan's most historic and culturally rich cities. Samarkand is a place where stunning Islamic architecture meets bustling bazaars and centuries of history. This guide will help you discover the best attractions, foods, and cultural experiences. Marvel at the jaw-dropping architecture, archaeological treasures, and vast history of Samarkand, listed as the Crossroads of Cultures in the UNESCO World Heritage. Admire the sacred necropolis house of Shah-I-Zinda’s jaw-dropping tilework that dates back to the 14th century Timurid Empire. Marvel at the splendid and magnificent architecture of Gur-E-Amir, which inspired the Taj Mahal in India, and step inside to see the tombs of Amir Timur, the founder of the Timurid Empire and his sons and grandsons.

From ancient madrasas to traditional markets, Samarkand has something for everyone. Visit Registan square, admire the intricate mosaics at Shah-i-Zinda, or explore the Gur-e-Amir mausoleum. Don't forget to try local dishes like plov and shashlik, and immerse yourself in the vibrant culture of this historic city.

Fun Facts About Samarkand

  • Samarkand is over 2,500 years old and was a key stop on the Silk Road.
  • The Registan square was once the heart of the ancient city and a center for education and trade.
  • Samarkand’s blue-tiled mosques and madrasas are considered some of the most beautiful Islamic architecture in the world.
